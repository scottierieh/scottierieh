Normality Tests

Assess if data follows a normal distribution

Normality is a fundamental assumption for many parametric tests including t-tests, ANOVA, and linear regression. These tests help determine whether your data approximates a normal (Gaussian) distribution, which is crucial for the validity of statistical inferences.

Homogeneity Tests

Check equality of variances across groups

Homogeneity of variance (homoscedasticity) is a critical assumption for ANOVA, t-tests, and regression analysis. These tests verify whether different groups or conditions have similar variance, ensuring the validity of statistical comparisons and model assumptions.
