Difference Tests

Compare groups, means, and distributions to detect statistically significant differences

🎯 When to Use Difference Tests?

Difference tests help you determine if observed differences between groups are statistically significant or could have occurred by chance. Choose the appropriate test based on your data type, sample size, and distribution assumptions.

T-Tests

Compare means between groups or against a value

T-tests are fundamental parametric tests for comparing means. They're ideal for continuous data that follows a normal distribution and when you need to compare sample means to population values, or compare means between two groups.

ANOVA

Analysis of variance for multiple group comparisons

Analysis of Variance (ANOVA) tests whether there are significant differences among three or more group means. It's the extension of t-tests for multiple groups and includes one-way, two-way, and repeated measures designs.

Chi-Square Tests

Test relationships in categorical data

Chi-square tests examine relationships between categorical variables. They test whether observed frequencies differ significantly from expected frequencies, making them essential for analyzing contingency tables and goodness-of-fit assessments.

Non-parametric Tests

Distribution-free tests for robust analysis

Non-parametric tests make no assumptions about data distribution, making them robust alternatives when parametric assumptions are violated. They work with ranks and are ideal for skewed data, small samples, or ordinal variables.
